# Mental Health Care Access — California Counties

**Live analysis:** [https://khushi-2020.github.io/mental-health-access-ca](https://khushi-2020.github.io/mental-health-access-ca)

A county-level analysis of mental health care access across California using the *Two Dimensions of Access* dataset (OSF, v1.0, January 2026). Provider data is drawn from the Psychology Today public directory.

---

## Five Key Insights

1. **Urban Medicaid acceptance is effectively zero** — Only 1.3% of urban providers accept Medicaid vs. 13.0% in micropolitan counties (10× gap). For 38.8 million urban Californians, the public insurance pathway to mental health care is statistically nonexistent.

2. **Rural counties charge the highest fees despite lower incomes** — Rural median session fee ($167.50) exceeds urban ($160.00), yet rural disposable income is $11,500 lower, pushing the fee burden to 4.9% of income vs. 3.8% urban.

3. **Fee burden reaches ~10% in the most constrained counties** — Sierra County leads at 9.7%; counties below $25K disposable income consistently spend 6–10% on 10 sessions.

4. **Access deserts cluster in the Northern Interior and Central Valley** — Choropleth maps of AVI (providers per 100K residents) and AFI (10-session cost as % of income) reveal geographic concentration of low-access areas.

5. **Policy and practice implications** — Urban Medicaid reform, rural workforce incentives, and targeted investment in geographic access deserts each address distinct failure modes that the data surfaces clearly.

## Data

**Source:** Two Dimensions of Access dataset  
**Citation:** *Two Dimensions of Access*, v1.0, January 2026. Available at: [https://osf.io/6ezrx](https://osf.io/6ezrx)

> ⚠️ The dataset is not included in this repository. To reproduce the analysis, download the CSV from OSF and place it in the `data/` folder.

**Coverage:** 57 of 58 California counties (Modoc County excluded — no listed providers).

## Reproduce the Analysis

### Requirements

- R ≥ 4.2
- Packages: `tidyverse`, `here`, `ggrepel`, `scales`

### Setup

```r
install.packages(c("tidyverse", "here", "ggrepel", "scales"))
```

### Run

```r
source("R/analysis.R")
```

Figures are saved to `figures/` (created automatically on first run).
